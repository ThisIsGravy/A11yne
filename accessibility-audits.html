Accessibility Audits

Accessibility audits are an essential process for ensuring that your website or digital content is usable by everyone, including people with disabilities. These audits help identify and address barriers that prevent people from accessing content effectively. By conducting regular accessibility audits, you can ensure that your site complies with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) and provides an inclusive experience for all users.

Our accessibility audits include a comprehensive review of your website, covering key areas such as:

  • Ensuring all images have descriptive alt text
  • Testing keyboard navigation to make sure all elements are accessible without a mouse
  • Evaluating color contrast to guarantee text readability
  • Reviewing ARIA (Accessible Rich Internet Applications) roles and attributes for enhanced compatibility with assistive technologies
  • Assessing forms to verify they are properly labeled and provide clear error messages
  • Checking for consistent focus indicators on interactive elements

Our goal is to make your digital products accessible to all users, no matter their abilities. By conducting these audits, you can improve user satisfaction, meet legal requirements, and enhance your brand's reputation for inclusivity.

The Cost Benefits of Accessibility Audits

Investing in accessibility audits is not only about compliance and inclusivity—it is also a smart financial decision. The costs associated with an accessibility audit are typically far smaller than the costs of facing a lawsuit due to non-compliance. Lawsuits related to accessibility can lead to significant financial losses, including legal fees, settlements, and fines. In addition to direct costs, lawsuits can also cause considerable damage to your brand's reputation, resulting in lost customers and negative public relations.

The operational costs of addressing accessibility issues reactively, under the pressure of legal action, are often much higher compared to proactive measures. Remediating accessibility issues after a lawsuit can involve rushed fixes, increased development costs, and disruptions to your operations. By conducting regular accessibility audits, you can avoid these reactive expenses and ensure that your website is accessible from the start, leading to smoother operations and better user experiences.

Ultimately, investing in accessibility audits saves time, minimizes operational disruptions, and protects your brand from negative publicity, making it a cost-effective choice in the long run. We also want to help show you how you can end up doing the audits yourself to help build the culture within your organization.
